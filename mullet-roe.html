Sardinian Mullet Bottarga – Mediterranean Gold

Have you ever tasted an ingredient that tells the story of the Sardinian sea in a single bite? Sardinian Mullet Bottarga is a culinary treasure, also known as the “poor man’s caviar”, with its golden hue and intense aroma of sea and sun. The name “bottarga” comes from the Arabic būṭarikh, derived from the Greek ᾠοτάριχον (ōotárikhon), meaning “salted and dried fish eggs” – a Mediterranean delicacy passed down through centuries.

Tradition and Craftsmanship

Our artisanal bottarga is crafted by the expert hands of local fishermen and salt workers. Only selected mullet roe, sea salt, and time: no preservatives or additives. Slowly dried using traditional methods passed down through generations, preserving its unique aroma, texture, and flavor. Rich in high-quality proteins, omega-3 fatty acids (DHA & EPA), and fat-soluble vitamins A, D, and E, bottarga is not only delicious but supports cardiovascular health and overall well-being.

A Gourmet Experience for True Sea Lovers

Our Bottarga releases an intense and enveloping flavor, perfect grated over spaghetti with garlic and extra virgin olive oil, with traditional Sardinian bread, or as flakes on salads and appetizers. It’s more than an ingredient: it’s a journey through the Mediterranean, a small luxury telling the story of the sea and sun.
